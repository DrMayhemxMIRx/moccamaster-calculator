Why the lines don't mean what they say

Moccamaster's printed chart covers their whole product line, not just your machine — Cup-One is a separate single-serve brewer, not a setting on your KBGV, so it's left off here. On your machine, the "4-Cup" line (16oz) made exactly 1 real mug when you tested it — that's the actual ratio used throughout this calculator, not a generic assumption.

The scoop isn't a regular tablespoon

The Moccamaster scoop that came with your machine is sized so that 1 level scoop = 2 tbsp exactly, per their own spec. Use that scoop, not a kitchen tablespoon eyeballed twice.

Brew Steps

  1. Power switch off.
  2. Attach outlet arm to the reservoir, snug fit.
  3. Fill reservoir with fresh, cool water to your target line above. Replace lid.
  4. Place a No. 4 filter in brew basket, rinse with water.
  5. Add ground coffee — use the scoop/gram count above.
  6. Brew basket onto bracket, lid on, outlet arm centered over it.
  7. Carafe into position on the hotplate.
  8. Set the Brew-Volume Selector to match (small icon = 4–6 cup, large icon = 8–10 cup).
  9. Plug into grounded outlet, power switch ON.
  10. Brewing starts immediately — water boils, rises through the arm, pulses into the basket. Takes about 4–6 minutes for a full pot.

Best Practices

  • Grind size: medium to medium-coarse. Most pre-ground bagged coffee is too fine for this machine — grind at home or have it ground fresh for drip at purchase.
  • Filters: No. 4, oxygen-whitened. Skip gold-mesh/reusable filters — Moccamaster specifically advises against them for inconsistent extraction and difficult cleaning.
  • Water: fresh tap, filtered, or bottled is fine. Avoid distilled or reverse-osmosis water — it can damage the boiling element over time.
  • If coffee's too strong: add hot water to the finished cup. If too weak: don't add more grounds — switch roast or origin instead. That's Moccamaster's own guidance, not just a preference.
  • Minimum brew volume: don't brew under 16oz (4 cups) on this machine — smaller amounts don't extract evenly.
  • High altitude: water boils cooler at elevation (as low as 198°F vs. 212°F at sea level), so brews run slightly cooler too. Normal, not a malfunction.

Cleaning & Descaling

  • After every brew: hand-wash carafe and brew basket with mild dish soap, rinse well. Carafes aren't dishwasher safe.
  • Never use: abrasive cleaners, steel wool, or a bottle brush on the carafe — can scratch the glass liner and lead to breakage.
  • Exterior: wipe down with a moist cloth regularly.
  • Descale every 100 brew cycles (roughly every box of 100 filters) or at minimum every 3 months. Use Urnex® or Durgol® descaling products — follow their directions.
  • Do not use vinegar to descale — Moccamaster specifically advises against it; it doesn't fully remove buildup and can leave a lingering odor that taints future brews.
  • After descaling: run 2 full reservoirs of plain water through the machine to clear out any remaining descaling product before brewing coffee again.
  • Storing for 1+ week or off-season: unplug, remove the outlet arm, empty remaining water by holding the brewer upside down over the sink, then replace the arm before next use.

Beans & Grinding

  • Grind setting: medium, similar to coarse sand or kosher salt — matches the "medium to medium-coarse" Moccamaster recommends.
  • Roast level: medium roast, not light or dark. Light roasts run acidic/sour; dark roasts can taste bitter or burnt.
  • Origin: favor Central/South American beans (Colombia, Brazil, Guatemala, Honduras) for a smooth, low-acid, chocolatey cup. East African origins (Ethiopia, Kenya) lean bright and acidic — skip these if you don't like sourness.
  • Tasting notes to look for: chocolate, caramel, nutty, brown sugar, smooth, balanced. Avoid: bright, juicy, floral, winey, citrus, berry — all signal high acid.
  • Freshness: buy whole bean, check for a roast date (not "best by") within 1–2 weeks. Grind right before brewing — pre-ground coffee stales fast and can't be fixed by anything downstream.
  • Storage: airtight, opaque container, room temp, away from light. Not the fridge — condensation does more harm than the cold helps.
  • Buy small: what you'll drink in 2–3 weeks, not a big bag that sits open for months.
